# The Pudding: data-essay journalism + open datasets

**Main link:** <https://pudding.cool/>

Datasets repo: <https://github.com/the-pudding/data>

## Summary

[The Pudding](https://pudding.cool/) is a digital publication that produces *data essays* — long-form, scrolly, heavily-visualised pieces on cultural and quantitative topics ("How women's pockets compare to men's", "The differing diversity of musical genres", "How rappers' vocabularies stack up against Shakespeare"). It's run by a small editorial team and is one of the very best places on the open web to study what genuinely good interactive data journalism looks like in 2024. As a bonus, the underlying datasets for many of the essays are released as CSVs in their public [`the-pudding/data`](https://github.com/the-pudding/data) repo.

## Insight

Two reasons to bookmark this site:

1. **As an inspiration source.** Almost every essay does *one* visualisation idea unusually well — small multiples, scrollytelling, comparative timelines, force-directed graphs of artist collaborations, etc. Steal techniques, not topics.
2. **As a free dataset library.** The repo includes the rapper-vocabulary corpus, the men's vs. women's pocket measurements, songs-of-the-year data, etc. — all niche, all clean, all interesting starting points if you want to learn a new visualisation tool against real data instead of the iris dataset for the thousandth time.

The team writes openly about their process; if you want to develop your own taste, read their [retrospective posts](https://pudding.cool/process/) — these are some of the best public write-ups on how interactive editorial pieces actually get made.
