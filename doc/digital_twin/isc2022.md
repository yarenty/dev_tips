# ISC 2022 — supercomputing & digital-twin keynote (NVIDIA + BMW)

**Main link:** <https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/2022/05/30/isc-digital-twins/>

## Summary

Opening keynote of **ISC High Performance 2022** (Hamburg, May 2022), jointly delivered by **Rev Lebaredian** (NVIDIA, VP Omniverse and Simulation Technology) and **Michele Melchiorre** (BMW Group, SVP Production System). Thesis: a new class of physically-accurate, real-time, planet-scale digital twins is now feasible because three things converged — supercomputer-class GPU compute, AI-physics methods (PINNs, neural operators), and standardised scene representation (USD). Lebaredian framed the "superpowers" digital twins unlock (teleport, time-travel into the past via recorded state, time-travel to predicted futures); Melchiorre showed BMW's iFACTORY as a concrete instance.

## Insight

This talk is the **2022 inflection-point reference** for the "industrial-scale digital twin" narrative. Worth reading not for any single technical insight — most of it is vendor framing — but as a shared vocabulary touchstone everyone in the space is working off. The four substantive points worth carrying forward:

1. **Precision-time networking** is the bottleneck. Multiple autonomous systems sharing one space-time means PTP, low-latency 5G, and deterministic schedulers — not just GPUs.
2. **USD (Universal Scene Description)** is becoming the de-facto interchange format for industrial-scale 3D + simulation data. Anything claiming digital-twin interop should speak USD.
3. **Physics-informed ML** (NVIDIA Modulus and friends — see [[nvidia_omniverse]]) is the bridge between traditional simulation and observed sensor streams. It's how you keep the twin "actually predictive".
4. The **OEM-supplier model** for sims (auto industry has hundreds of partners on Omniverse) is the template other industries will follow.

### "Superpowers" Lebaredian listed

| Power | What it actually means |
|-------|------------------------|
| **Teleportation** | Anyone can join the simulated world from anywhere, like a multiplayer game; engineers don't have to be on-site. |
| **Time travel (past)** | If you record the world's state continuously, you can scrub the timeline back to any moment and re-explore it. |
| **Time travel (future)** | Run the predictive simulator forward; explore *many* possible futures, not just one. Useful for city planning, climate modelling, factory layouts. |

### Required ingredients (per the talk)

- **GPU-accelerated compute** at data-centre scale (NVIDIA OVX servers were the ISC announcement).
- **Precision Time Protocol (PTP)** networking with low latency at the edge.
- **Continuous sensor sync** between the physical and digital halves.
- **Physics-accurate sim** (Omniverse + Modulus + USD).
- **AI-augmented simulation** so the twin can extrapolate beyond what's been measured.
